Brand Strategy · Messaging Framework

Brand Messaging Framework

Tone of voice & positioning document — EdTech SaaS brand, B2B corporate learning audience
Developed for an EdTech SaaS company serving corporate L&D teams. This extract defines core voice pillars and the messaging hierarchy for use across all communication channels.
Brand
LearnForge — Corporate Learning Platform
Positioning Statement
"LearnForge helps L&D teams build workforces that learn faster than the market changes. We replace one-size-fits-all training with intelligent, adaptive learning pathways that connect to real business outcomes — not just completion rates."
Voice & Tone Pillars
Authoritative, not academic
We speak from experience, not theory. Our language is confident and direct — we make claims we can back up and avoid jargon that obscures rather than clarifies.
Human, not corporate
L&D professionals deal with enough stiff, impersonal communication. We write the way a knowledgeable colleague talks — clearly, warmly, without pretending software alone changes culture.
Precise, not minimal
We don't pad. Every sentence earns its place. We choose the specific word over the general one, and the concrete example over the abstract claim.
Messaging Hierarchy by Audience
Audience Primary Need Lead Message
CLO / L&D Director Business outcomes, ROI, strategic alignment "Turn learning data into leadership decisions."
HR Business Partner Adoption, employee engagement, retention "Learning that employees actually use — and managers actually see."
IT / Procurement Security, integration, scalability "Enterprise-grade infrastructure. Implementation in days, not months."

Executive Communications · Internal Announcement

Executive Communication

CEO internal message — organisational structure update · Clear, confident, and human
Written on behalf of a CEO communicating a strategic reorganisation to the full company. Tone: clear and confident — acknowledging uncertainty while maintaining forward momentum.
TO:All Team Members
FROM:Khaled Al-Mansouri, Chief Executive Officer
DATE:March 2026
SUBJECT:A New Chapter — Evolving How We Work Together

Team,

I want to share something important with you — and I want to share it directly, without the kind of corporate language that turns real news into a press release.

Over the past few months, we have grown faster than any of us anticipated. That growth is something to be proud of. It is also, as many of you have felt firsthand, something that has put strain on how we work together. Decisions have taken longer. Ownership has become less clear. Information has not always reached the people who need it most.

Starting in April, we are reorganising into four focused business units: Commercial, Delivery, Product, and People & Operations. Each unit will have a dedicated lead with clear accountability and direct access to leadership. This is not a restructuring driven by cost — it is a structure built for the next stage of growth.

What this means for you, practically:

  • Your direct reporting line may change — your manager will connect with you individually by Friday.
  • Team leads will host open Q&A sessions next week — dates and links will follow from your unit lead.
  • Nothing changes in how we serve our clients. Our commitments stand.

I know that change — even positive change — can feel unsettling. I do not expect you to be immediately enthusiastic about this. I do ask that you come with questions, and that you give the new structure a fair chance to show what it can do.

What has made this company work is not any structure on paper. It is the quality of the people in this room — and that does not change.

With appreciation,
Khaled Al-Mansouri
Chief Executive Officer
